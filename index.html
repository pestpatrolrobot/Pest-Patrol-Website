Our Mission

Engineering a Sustainable Tomorrow

Pest Patrol was born from a singular vision: to bridge the gap between manual pesticide application and practical automation. Our system is designed as a mobile spraying car that helps farmers apply treatment more efficiently.

Efficiency

Supporting consistent spraying while reducing unnecessary pesticide use.

Reliability

Consistent performance across all terrain conditions.

Why Pest Patrol?

"In an era where food security is paramount, we focus on a practical automated pesticide car that can help protect crops with controlled movement and spraying."

- The Engineering Council

Project Parts

Components We Used

These are the main electronics, mechanical parts, power components, and spraying materials used to build the Pest Patrol prototype.

Control and Electronics

Boards and modules that control movement, sensing, switching, and automation.

Controller
Arduino Uno R3

Main microcontroller used for basic logic, signal handling, and component control.

Controller
ESP32

Compact control board that can support wireless features and additional automation logic.

Switching
Relay Module

Switches higher-power parts like the pump from low-power control signals.

Motor Driver
Motor Driver Module

Controls motor direction and speed so the chassis can move properly.

Servo Control
Servo Channel Module

Helps manage servo movement for positioning and controlled mechanical motion.

Sensors and Movement

Parts that help the prototype detect surroundings, follow paths, and move as a vehicle.

Sensor
Ultrasonic Sensor

Measures distance so the robot can detect nearby objects or obstacles.

Sensor
Line Tracking Sensor

Supports path-following tests by detecting dark or light guide lines.

Chassis
4WD Chassis Kit

Vehicle base that carries the electronics, pump, container, and sprayer setup.

Mechanism
4DOF Arm

Mechanical arm used for positioning or adjusting the spray direction during testing.

Spraying System

Water and pesticide flow parts used to move liquid from the container to the nozzle.

Flow
Pump

Pushes liquid through the tube so pesticide can be sprayed from the nozzle.

Spray
Nozzle

Shapes the liquid output into a spray pattern for crop application.

Flow
Tube

Carries liquid from the tank or pump toward the spray nozzle.

Fitting
Barb Connector

Connects tubes securely so the liquid path stays stable during operation.

Power Supply

Battery and voltage parts that provide usable power for the robot.

Battery
NCR Battery

Rechargeable power source for the mobile prototype.

Holder
Battery Holder

Keeps batteries mounted and connected safely to the circuit.

Voltage
Buck Converter

Steps voltage down to a safer level for boards and modules.

Voltage
Secondary Converter

Provides another regulated output for parts that need a different voltage level.

Switch
Rocker Switch

Provides a simple physical on/off control for the system.

Wiring and Assembly

Connection and mounting materials used to assemble the prototype cleanly.

Wiring
Jumper Wires

Connects modules and sensors during prototyping and testing.

Wiring
AWG Wire

Used for longer or stronger electrical connections inside the robot.

Mounting
M3 Standoffs

Raises and secures boards or panels with clean spacing.

Mounting
M3 Bolts and Nuts

Fasteners used to attach parts to the chassis and support structure.
